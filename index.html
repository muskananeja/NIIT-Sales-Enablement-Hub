The arc

How twelve engagements happened.

2021

Digital learning brand platform

First engagement. A discrete project — scope-defined, no follow-on assumed. NIIT delivered something specific and visible.

What this taught usThe first engagement is a credentialing event, not a relationship. Deliver something that other people inside the client will hear about.
2022

Digital LBP extension · Academy work begins

The first project's visibility opened two adjacent conversations. Same sponsor, broader scope.

What this taught usStay close after delivery. The natural next conversation usually surfaces within 90 days — if someone is asking for it.
2023

Cedric brought in as senior NIIT relationship

A consulting director was anchored to the account — not tied to any one project. Conversations moved from execution to strategy.

What this taught usSenior anchoring matters. A CD on the account creates a reason to talk that isn't dependent on an active SOW.
2024

Multi-stakeholder workshops · New sponsors emerge

In-person workshops broadened the stakeholder map beyond the original sponsor. When the original sponsor moved roles, the relationship survived.

What this taught usSingle-sponsor accounts are fragile. Use workshops and steering committees to make the relationship redundant of any one person.
2025

Adjacent clusters opened · Portfolio & AI work

The client expanded NIIT's remit into Portfolio Optimization and AI capability. Two new consulting directors involved — the relationship had become institutional.

What this taught usExpansion happens through adjacency, not through reselling. New work comes from naming a problem we're seeing in their environment, not from pitching another offering.
What sustained it

Six conditions to repeat
on every strategic account.

01

Demonstrated enterprise capability

Visible project success in a high-stakes environment built credibility that extended beyond the original engagement.

02

Multi-stakeholder relationship depth

Relationships anchored to a single sponsor are vulnerable. UCB grew when we built connections across functions and leadership layers.

03

Senior-led, problem-first engagement

Early CD involvement and conversations led through business challenges — not service pitches — created broader access faster.

04

Continuity of knowledge

Retained context about client systems, governance, and stakeholder history meant we re-engaged faster than competitors could even pitch.

05

Integrated advisory & delivery

Clients valued our ability to move from strategy to execution. Advisory without delivery follow-through rarely generated repeat work.

06

Sustained visibility between engagements

Regular touchpoints grounded in delivered outcomes and relevant insight kept us connected to future transformation agendas during quiet periods.

How the site works

The logic, the email flow,
and how we'll measure it.

For sales enablement, account directors, and anyone deploying this internally. Three things: how the account router decides what to recommend, how an email gets drafted and sent from inside this site, and what to track once it goes live.

Part one · Routing logic

How the account router decides.

Twelve rules, evaluated top to bottom. The first rule that matches wins — and routes to a single recommended move. The order matters because most accounts will match more than one rule.

01
Active consulting engagementRelationship = Active consulting (regardless of other inputs)
Have the "what comes next" conversation before closeAlready in Tier 1. Convert this engagement into the next one before momentum closes.
02
Active Managed ServicesRelationship = Active Managed Services. The warm path from delivery into consulting.
Activate consulting from within MSHighest-leverage entry into Tier 3 — the MS team already has access and trust.
03
Dormant + specific signalRelationship = Dormant consulting AND signal field has 10+ characters of context
Return with a perspective on their workThe signal is the substance — engage with it directly.
04
Dormant + leadership changeRelationship = Dormant consulting AND Pressure = Leadership change / M&A / restructure
Engage a new stakeholder where the sponsor moved onApproach the new mandate-holder with our existing organisational context.
05
Dormant — all other casesRelationship = Dormant consulting AND no signal AND no leadership change
Introduce a senior NIIT relationshipBring a CD in as a standalone reason to reconnect — not tied to a project.
06
Past MS onlyRelationship = Past Managed Services (closed)
Activate consulting from within MSThe MS context is still recent enough to be the warm path back in.
07
Cold + specific signalRelationship = Cold AND signal field has 10+ characters
Use a publicly stated prioritySpecific signals beat generic outreach. Engage with what you've heard.
08
Cold + AI workforce pressureRelationship = Cold AND Pressure = AI capability across the workforce
Anchor outreach to a sector inflectionAI is the universal C-suite ask — a sharp NIIT view earns a 30-minute call.
09
Cold + leadership eventRelationship = Cold AND Pressure = Leadership change / M&A / restructure
Anchor outreach to a sector inflectionInflection points reset what an L&D function needs to be.
10
Cold + "don't know yet"Relationship = Cold AND Pressure = Don't know yet
Offer the Learning Function ReviewIf neither party can yet name the problem, the LFR diagnostic is the right starting point.
11
Cold + cluster pressureCold + Pressure = Skills / Operating model / Portfolio / Measurement / Tech / Academy
Use a publicly stated priorityConnect their stated priority to NIIT's relevant thinking. Expert-to-expert, not vendor-to-buyer.
12
Cold + no signals (fallback)Cold and no other inputs strong enough to trigger a more specific rule
Share the 2026 Global Learning BenchmarkClean, non-commercial entry point that builds context for the first call.

Rules can be tuned by editing the generateBrief() function. The full logic sits in one place — readable, no library dependencies. As real account data accumulates, the ordering can be re-prioritised based on conversion rates by rule.

Part two · Email flow

How an email gets drafted, sent, and tracked.

From the moment a salesperson opens a move to the moment they hit send. Every step is designed to be repeatable and to leave a trace.

The five-step send

01 · ROUTE
Find the move
Either via the account router on the homepage, or by browsing tier pages.
02 · REVIEW
Open the move detail
Read when-to-use, why-it-works, what-to-leave-behind, four-step approach.
03 · EDIT
Replace placeholders
Pink-highlighted fields signal where to swap in the account specifics.
04 · COPY
Copy the template
"Copy template" button puts the full subject+body on the clipboard.
05 · SEND + LOG
Paste in mail client, log in CRM
Salesperson sends from their own mailbox. Logs the move ID + account in CRM.
For the salesperson

The editing logic

  • ·Every template has 4–8 {placeholder} fields — pink-highlighted in the UI
  • ·Common fields: {Name}, {company}, {sector}, {project}
  • ·Move-specific fields: {specific observation}, {CD Name}, {specific event}
  • ·Replacing all placeholders is the only required edit — the body stays intact
If we host this internally

Deployment options

  • ·Static site — single HTML file, hosted on SharePoint or internal CDN. Zero infrastructure.
  • ·CRM-integrated — wrap the same logic in a Salesforce / HubSpot lightning component. Adds account context auto-fill.
  • ·Outlook add-in — turns the email template into a draft directly inside the salesperson's mailbox.
  • ·Recommendation for v1: static deployment, manual CRM logging.
Part three · Measurement

What to track once it's live.

Four metrics that matter — and where the data comes from. The point isn't to surveil sellers; it's to learn which moves actually convert.

Usage

Is anyone using it?

  • ·Pageviews per tier landing page
  • ·Move pages opened per week
  • ·"Copy template" button clicks (by move)
  • ·Account router submissions per week

Source: client-side analytics (Plausible, GA, or simple event log).

Conversion

Which moves actually land?

  • ·Emails sent (logged in CRM with move ID)
  • ·Replies received (CRM thread tracking)
  • ·Meetings booked (calendar / CRM)
  • ·Reply rate per move (the headline metric)

Source: CRM. Requires salespeople to log "move sent" against each outreach.

Outcome

Did it generate revenue?

  • ·Meetings → qualified opportunities
  • ·Opportunities → won engagements
  • ·Revenue per move (over a 6-month attribution window)
  • ·Tier movement: Tier 3 → Tier 2 → Tier 1 transitions

Source: CRM opportunity stage data + SOW system.

Quality

Is the logic right?

  • ·Router recommendation acceptance rate (did seller open the move?)
  • ·Override rate (did seller pick a different move?)
  • ·Reply rates by recommended-vs-self-selected move
  • ·Salesperson feedback on tier definitions and move clarity

Source: site analytics + quarterly EM/CD interviews.

Recommended pilot

8 weeks. 10 EMs. Three tiers. One simple dashboard.

Deploy the site to a small group, require move-ID logging in CRM for every outreach sent through it, and review the dashboard weekly. By week 8 we should see which moves convert, where the router is wrong, and which tier definitions need sharpening before broader rollout.
